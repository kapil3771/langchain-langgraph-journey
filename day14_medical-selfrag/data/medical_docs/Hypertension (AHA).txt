What is High Blood Pressure?
-----------------------------
- Blood pressure (BP) is measured as systolic/diastolic (e.g., 112/78 mm Hg).
- Normal: < 120/80 mm Hg; Elevated: 120–129/<80; High BP (hypertension): ≥130/≥80 mm Hg  [oai_citation:0‡heart.org](https://www.heart.org/-/media/files/health-topics/answers-by-heart/what-is-high-blood-pressure.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com).
- Often has no symptoms, hence called a “silent killer.” Regular checks are essential  [oai_citation:1‡heart.org](https://www.heart.org/-/media/files/health-topics/answers-by-heart/what-is-high-blood-pressure.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com).
- Risk factors: age, genetics, overweight, high salt intake, inactivity, alcohol, smoking  [oai_citation:2‡who.int](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/hypertension?utm_source=chatgpt.com).
- Complications include heart attack, stroke, kidney disease, vision loss  [oai_citation:3‡who.int](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/hypertension?utm_source=chatgpt.com).
- Management: lifestyle changes (diet, exercise, weight loss, stress reduction) plus medication as advised by a doctor  [oai_citation:4‡en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypertension?utm_source=chatgpt.com).
